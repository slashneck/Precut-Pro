# Precut Pro

**Precut Pro** is a Windows desktop app that gives precuts a **proper home**—not a messy Drive folder or an endless Discord scroll. Browse **by anime** or **across everything**, use **tags and filters** to find what you mean, and keep **favorites** in one place so opening precuts feels **intentional**, not like file archaeology.

---

## Download

Get the latest **Windows x64** build from [**Releases**](https://github.com/slashneck/Precut-Pro/releases).  
The self-contained package does **not** require a separate .NET install.

---

## What you can do

- Explore the catalog **by show** or use a **flat precut list** with search
- **Preview** clips in the app (with sensible handling for restricted or spoiler-tagged content)
- Use **tags and metadata** to narrow down clips
- **Favorite** precuts and jump back to them quickly
- **Download** clips when you want files on disk
- See **recently uploaded** highlights
- **Check for updates** when new releases are published on GitHub

---

## Requirements

- **Windows 10/11** (64-bit)

---

## Security & privacy

- The app is built for **browsing and managing precuts** from your catalog. It does **not** rifle through unrelated personal folders.
- **Windows SmartScreen** may warn on first launch for **new or unsigned** software. That is normal until the app is **code-signed** with a trusted certificate.

---

## Known issues (short)

- **SmartScreen / “untrusted”** warnings can appear (unsigned build, no EV cert yet).
- **Performance**: loading **many** items at once (especially on the **Precuts** tab / search) can **briefly freeze** the UI—largely due to **Google Drive** round-trips. Prefer other views for the smoothest ride until improved. Minor freeze can occur when **favoriting** a single precut.
- **Recently Uploaded**: duration shown there may **not match** the real length; details in **dedicated anime folder** views are more reliable.
- **Sorting**: not every precut is fully ordered everywhere yet; this improves over time and should **refresh on your side** as metadata updates.
